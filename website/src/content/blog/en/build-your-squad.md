FOM is more fun with your people in it. The friend system turns a list of names into an actual squad — one you can pull from instantly, track against, and climb the rankings with.

## Adding friends

Send a friend request, they accept, and they're on your list. From there you can see their profile: their MMR, their tier, their win rate, and how they've been trending. It's a friendly way to keep score off the court too.

## Why it's worth doing

- **Faster match setup.** Instead of typing names every week, pull your regulars straight from your friends list in a couple of taps.
- **Real rivalries.** Watching a friend's MMR creep past yours is exactly the kind of motivation that gets people back on court.
- **Your crew in one place.** No more reconstructing the group from memory every session.

## Quick-add during setup

When you're building a match, your friends show up right there in the player picker. Tap to add, and you're done. You can even filter by MMR if you're trying to keep the teams balanced — or deliberately unbalanced, if you're feeling brave.

## The bigger picture

Friends, rankings, and Rooms all feed each other. Your squad shows up in your Rooms, your matches feed everyone's MMR, and the leaderboard turns your group into a running competition that lasts way beyond any single night.

Add a few friends before your next session and watch how quickly "let's play sometime" turns into a standing weekly game.
